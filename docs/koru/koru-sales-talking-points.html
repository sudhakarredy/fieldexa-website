Koru Sales Talking Points

Scripts, customer conversations, objection handlers, and competitive positioning for your sales team

🎯 The 30-Second Opening Pitch

"Your biggest operational pain point isn't crop health. It's harvest unpredictability."

You plan logistics, procurement, and buyer commitments weeks ahead. But harvest timing stays unpredictable. That variability costs you 15-30% in logistics waste, quality losses, and buyer misalignment.

Koru solves that. We forecast harvest dates, grades, and logistics needs 7+ days ahead. Farm-level precision. Rule-based (explainable). Improving with your data (Gen-1 → Gen-2 → Gen-3).

The result? Better planning. Less waste. Stronger buyer relationships. Measurable ROI from day one.

💬 Common Customer Conversations

Customer Pain: "Our harvests always surprise us"

"We can never predict when the peak harvest will be. We plan for X, but get Y. It's chaos for logistics and our buyers are frustrated."
Koru's Answer: "Harvest timing depends on GDD (growing degree days), rainfall, stress levels, and crop genetics. These aren't random. Koru builds a model of exactly how these factors affect YOUR farm. Result? 7+ day visibility into harvest timing. Not guesses. Data-driven predictions."
Key Follow-up: "In your first month, you'll see how accurate Koru is. Month 2, you can start committing harvest dates to buyers with confidence. By month 3, your logistics planning is based on actual data, not hope."

Customer Need: "Our buyers need quality forecasts"

"When our buyers ask 'How much A-grade will I get?', we don't know. We guess. Then we miss commitments or oversupply."
Koru's Answer: "Koru forecasts not just WHEN you'll harvest, but also the GRADE MIX. Which percentage will be A, B, C grade. Our model learns how stress conditions, weather, and your specific field conditions affect grade distribution. You answer 'How much A-grade?' with actual forecasts, not guesses."
The Benefit: "Buyers respect predictability. Accurate grade forecasts → better contracts → stronger relationships → recurring volumes."

Customer Challenge: "Our teams work across 10 clusters"

"We have harvest predictions from 10 different regions. Coordinating vehicles, labor, and buyer deliveries is impossible without a single view."
Koru's Answer: "Koru is built for exactly this. Geo-scoped filtering by State, Area, Cluster, Village. You can drill from the national view down to a single farm. Weekly distribution engine allocates harvest across crops and regions. Your operations team sees everything. Makes decisions from one dashboard."

Customer Risk: "We've had duplicate farmer payments"

"Same farmer, different IDs. We caught it too late. Paid twice. Audit nightmare. How do you prevent that?"
Koru's Answer: "Fuzzy Duplicate Checker. We match farmers on phone (exact match), name (75%+ fuzzy match), and village. Confidence scoring. If we detect a probable duplicate, we surface it to your finance team for approval. Automated detection + human judgment = zero duplicates + 100% audit trail."
The Win: "Finance team approves the system day one. Compliance risk eliminated. Audit-ready from launch."

Customer Concern: "Will farmers engage with alerts?"

"We send English alerts. Farmers ignore them. We don't know when stress happens. No visibility into actual conditions."
Koru's Answer: "Koru sends alerts in the farmer's native language via SMS/WhatsApp. Stress alerts, harvest timing, input issues—all in their language, on their channel. Higher engagement. Better data coming back. Your team gets better visibility, farmers get actionable information."

🛡️ Objection Handlers

🔴 "We already use weather data. Why do we need Koru?"

The Real Issue: Customer thinks Koru is just another weather app. You need to reframe it.

Your Response: "Weather is one input. But harvest timing also depends on crop genetics, sowing date, stress response, GDD timing, and YOUR farm's history. Koru integrates weather WITH all those factors. Weather tells us 'it rained'. Koru tells us 'it rained, so this field will mature 3 days later, and grades will shift 5% from A to B'. That's the difference between knowing conditions and predicting outcomes."

Close: "Let's show you the model in a quick demo. You'll see exactly what inputs drive harvest timing on YOUR farm."

🔴 "AI scares us. We don't trust black-box models"

The Real Issue: Customer fears automation, data dependency, lack of control.

Your Response: "We don't use black-box AI. Koru is 100% transparent. Gen-1 uses rules: GDD → Phenology → Stress → Grade adjustment. You see the rules. You can audit them. You can adjust them. As you collect data (Gen-2), we ADD Random Forest on top—but you still see the rules. Gen-3 is ensemble, but it's explainable. You never lose control."

Key Point: "Predictable vs Actual tracking shows you our accuracy daily. You're not betting on AI. You're betting on data visibility."

Close: "Your team, not algorithms, makes final decisions. Koru is decision support, not decision replacement."

🔴 "We need historical data to start. We don't have any"

The Real Issue: Customer thinks ML requires months of data. Koru's Gen-1 approach solves this.

Your Response: "Most platforms need 2+ years of historical data. Koru's Gen-1 model is rule-based. Deploy on DAY ONE. No waiting for data. Start getting predictions immediately. After 200 harvests, we evolve to Gen-2 (Random Forest learns from YOUR outcomes). By 500 harvest-seasons, you're at Gen-3 ensemble. ROI visible from month one."

The Benefit: "You're not paying for a data science project. You're deploying a working system that improves over time."

Close: "Show me a competitor who launches without historical data. We do."

🔴 "This is too complex. Our team can't use it"

The Real Issue: Customer fears adoption friction, training burden, system complexity.

Your Response: "Koru is designed for operations teams, not data scientists. Executive dashboard shows what you need to know (forecast, weekly plan). AI Copilot (Hola) answers questions in natural language. Configurable week logic means your business rules become the system's rules. No workarounds. Your team adopted it in 2 weeks (based on customer data)."

Close: "We'll handle training and onboarding. Your team will be productive day one. After a month, they'll wonder how they operated without it."

🔴 "How do we know this will work for OUR farm?"

The Real Issue: Customer wants proof before committing. Legitimate question.

Your Response: "Fair. That's why we do a 2-week pilot. Pick your largest farm or cluster. We ingest your data, build your model, show you predictions. You compare predictions vs actual results in real-time. If accuracy looks good, we expand. If not, you walked away early. Zero risk to you."

The Outcome: "Most pilots show 75%+ accuracy in week 2, 85%+ by week 4. You'll see confidence building as results match reality."

Close: "Predicted vs Actual tracking is your accountability mechanism. You're not guessing. You're seeing proof."

🔴 "Your competitor offered a lower price"

The Real Issue: Customer is choosing on price alone. Reframe on value.

Your Response: "Price is one factor. ROI is what matters. If Competitor X costs 30% less but gets accuracy wrong, you lose 2x more in logistics waste than you saved. Koru's transparent, progressive model means you're not locked into a black-box platform from day one. You can audit, verify, and adjust. You get accuracy you can trust. What's that worth? Usually 3-5x the platform cost."

Ask Discovery: "How much are you losing annually to harvest unpredictability?" (Typical answer: 10-30% of margin). "That's 20x the Koru cost."

Close: "Let's focus on ROI, not price. I think you'll find Koru pays for itself in 30 days."

🎯 Sales Closing Tactics

The Trial Close (2-Week Pilot)

"Let's run a 2-week pilot on your largest farm/cluster."

Why this works: Zero commitment. Proof of concept in real environment. Koru wins if accuracy is good.

What to show at end of pilot:

  • Predicted vs Actual harvest dates (accuracy %)
  • Grade mix forecasts vs actual grade distribution
  • Dashboard showing how your operations team will use it
  • ROI projection extrapolated from pilot farm

Typical Outcome: If pilot shows 80%+ accuracy, customer is 80% likely to expand to all farms.

The Budget Close

"What's your operations or supply chain budget for this year?"

Often, Koru costs 5-10% of that budget. And saves 20-30% of logistics costs. Easy math.

Reframe: "This isn't adding cost. This is redirecting existing budget from waste reduction to visibility."

The Authority Close

Identify decision-makers early:

  • Operations Head: Cares about logistics efficiency, daily planning
  • CFO / Finance Head: Cares about compliance, fraud prevention, measurable ROI
  • Procurement Head: Cares about buyer relationships, grade forecasting
  • IT / Tech Head: Cares about integration, data security, scalability

Tactic: Talk to each stakeholder about their specific pain point. Koru addresses all of them.

The Timeline Close

"Let's get you live before next season's peak harvest."

Why it works: Seasonal urgency. If you miss next harvest, you're waiting 6+ months for ROI.

What to promise: Pilot (weeks 1-2), Onboarding (weeks 3-4), Live (week 5)

The Partnership Close

"This is a partnership, not a vendor relationship."

Why it works: Large enterprises are tired of vendors. They want partners.

How to deliver: Weekly check-ins during first month. Config support for their specific week logic. Training for their team. Continuous improvement.

What to commit: "You're not paying for a static tool. You're paying for improving intelligence that gets better with your data."

📋 Quick Reference Checklist

Before Customer Calls

  • Know their farm size, geographies, crop types
  • Research their supply chain (who are their buyers?)
  • Identify their pain point (logistics, quality, compliance, or all three)
  • Prepare a 2-3 minute pitch aligned to their pain
  • Have ROI calculator ready on your phone

During Conversations

  • Lead with problem ("Harvest unpredictability costs you 20%"), not features
  • Ask discovery questions before pitching solutions
  • Use specific metrics from their geography if you have them
  • Show the 3-generation roadmap (demonstrates thinking and confidence)
  • Always mention pilot as a low-risk next step

Closing

  • Confirm all stakeholders are bought in (Ops, Finance, IT)
  • Define pilot scope clearly (which farms, how long, what success looks like)
  • Set weekly check-in cadence
  • Get signed pilot agreement before starting work
  • Plan "expand to all farms" conversation for week 2 of pilot
Remember: You're not selling software. You're selling visibility and control. Koru is the tool that delivers those outcomes. Lead with the problem, build on pain points, and always tie features back to customer outcomes.
