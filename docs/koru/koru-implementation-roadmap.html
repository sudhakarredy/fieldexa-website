🚀 Koru Implementation Roadmap

Website Integration, Marketing Rollout & Sales Enablement Plan

Executive Overview

This roadmap ensures Koru is positioned, integrated, and sold effectively to prospects. Three key workstreams:

1Website Integration

Add Koru capabilities section to your product site. Reframed for executive and prospect audiences.

2Sales Enablement

Train sales team with talking points, objection handlers, ROI scripts, and competitive positioning.

3Marketing & Collateral

Create one-pagers, pitch decks, case studies, and buyer guides that drive inbound interest.

Timeline: Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2) → Phase 2 (Weeks 3-4) → Phase 3 (Weeks 5-8)

Resources Needed: Product marketing lead, sales ops, web development, content writer

⚡ Phase 1: Quick Wins (Weeks 1-2)

Get Koru visible and talking points in the hands of sales team immediately. Minimum viable integration.

Walk through the strategic positioning, 3-generation roadmap, and key talking points. Let them see the customer pain point (harvest unpredictability) and how Koru solves it.

Deliverable: Sales team equipped with Koru pitch, 3 main use cases, and objection handlers. Bookmark the talking-points HTML for reference.

4-6 hours dev time

Add a new "Koru" section or page to your product site. Use the capabilities-showcase HTML as the foundation. Link from main navigation.

Week 2 - Day 1-2

Sales Collateral (One-Pager)

2-3 hours content + design

Create a 1-page PDF summarizing Koru's value prop. Print and email. For sales to hand to prospects.

Deliverable: Downloadable one-pager (PDF) with problem statement, Koru solution, ROI, and CTA. Branded. Ready to email.

3-4 hours

🎯 Key Principles for Success

1. Lead with Problem, Not Features

Every conversation should start with "Harvest unpredictability costs you 15-30% margin loss." Then introduce Koru. Prospects will be more engaged if they feel the pain first.

2. The 3-Generation Roadmap Is Your Differentiator

This removes risk. Prospects see you're not betting on complex ML from day one. Transparent rules → evolving intelligence. Emphasize this in every conversation.

3. Predicted vs Actual Tracking Builds Trust

Show customers you're confident in your model. Transparency on accuracy (day 1, week 2, month 1 results) builds credibility. Include this in every pilot and demo.

4. Segment by Persona, Not Just Company Size

Operations teams care about logistics efficiency. Finance cares about fraud prevention. Procurement cares about buyer relationships. Use the same Koru, different angles for each persona.

5. SEO Matters—But Sales Enablement Matters More

Website traffic is great. But your sales team's ability to pitch is the conversion lever. Invest equally in both.

6. Measure Everything

Website traffic, email open rates, demo booking rate, proposal win rate, customer accuracy (Predicted vs Actual). Track these weekly. Optimize based on data, not assumptions.
